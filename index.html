Projects

Dynamics of Globular Clusters: The Effective Potential Through the Gaia Data Release 3
Master's thesis research focusing on internal dynamics of globular clusters, by reducing and analyzing data retrieved from the GAIA Data Release 3. After obtaining a 6-D phase space sample of the stars, kinetical analyses were carried out to extract the "Gravitational Effective Potential", an additional term to the single-star Hamiltonian that takes into account the dynamical gravitational friction between globular clusters and the host galaxy.
Link to thesis.

Simulation of a point-like and an extended source of SZ signal and filtering optimization for MUSTANG2
6 months research activity carried out during my Master's degree, in which, together with prof. Elia Battistelli as PI, we investigated the cosmic web through the study of the ICM (IntraCluster Medium) between colliding galaxy clusters Abel399 and Abel401. The observation of an extended Suniaev-Zel'dovich source was was simulated, recreating the Green Bank Telescope's 90GHz instrument MuSTANG2. Based on real noise data, synthetic data was created and added to the obtained simulated maps, and a filtering optimization analysis was carried out.
Link to exam paper.

Juno Data Analysis Workshop for Science Capacity Building
Workshop held by NASA and ASI in which I had the opportunity to work, together with Juno-NASA's expert scientists and engineers, with Jupiter's data from the latest spacecraft's close-orbits of the giant planet. In particular, my team's project focused on Jupyter's rings through the Stellar Reference Unit (SRU) instrument, particularly in measuring the intensity of the reflected light in function of viewing angle.
